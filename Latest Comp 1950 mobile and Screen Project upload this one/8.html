Web Development Methodology

What is a Methodology?

An organised, documented set of procedures and guidelines for one or more phases of the web development life cycle, such as analysis or design. A step-by-step "cookbook" approach for carrying out the procedure; and an objective set of criteria for determining whether the results of the procedure are of acceptable quality.

More than just Project Management, but it is derived from it.

There is a LOT to cover within Methodology, but it is critical that you have a good understanding of it.

Keys to remember

  • Many well-done projects will spend ~70% of your time planning!
  • Let your clients know this!
  • Results and "stuff to see" comes after considerable planning.

Why have a Methodology?

  • Predictable: With a solid methodology, you have a predictable set of processes and tasks that you, your team, and your clients can rely on.
  • Trackable: Progress is trackable, as you can look at each process or task and mark how much has been completed.
  • Responsibility: Assigning responsibility to each line item will allow you to work co-operatively with the client to maintain steady progress.
  • Teamwork: If you are working with a team, it is much easier to move forward if you have everyone subscribing to the same methodology.

What is your Methodology?

  • Every methodology is different - How do you put together your own web projects?
  • What is your methodology?
    • How do you approach a project?
    • Does it change depending on the size of the job?
    • Do you have defined phases?
    • What works well? What doesn't?
    • Don't have a lot of experience with web development projects? How do you think it should work?
  • Discuss your experiences with your group.
  • Determine some of the common tasks or phases
  • Try to produce a methodology that would allow you to complete a web project - from end to end
    • What needs to be involved?
    • What information needs to be gathered?
    • Client, contractor, team member relationships
    • a common set of rules that will guide you through each step of a complete project
  • Appoint a note-taker. Present your Methodology
